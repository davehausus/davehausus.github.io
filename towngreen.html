TOWN GREEN

Designing a tool to inform the community

OVERVIEW

The Town Green at Scotts Valley, California is a US$180 million mixed-use development that will feature housing, retail, restaurants, public art and green space. The entity formed to develop this project was in need of a website to serve as a channel to inform the Scotts Valley Community of what is getting approved.

GOAL

To develop a website that is mobile first in a period of 2 weeks (before the first event with the community). It is important to have the residents to submit their contact details so we can send them information as it becomes available.

ROLE

I was in charge of designing, developing and updating the website, as well as managing the subscriber database.

CASE TIMELINE

2018 - 2019 September

BUILDING FROM SCRATCH

With little to no information available at the moment, the first step was to design a mobile layout that could be seamlessly translated for a desktop experience. This layout will help the Team to visualize the possibilities the website could provide and the type of information that could be displayed.

MAKING VISIBLE SOMETHING THAT DOESN'T EXIST YET

As the Town Green doesn't exist yet, one challenge is the imagery to use to communicate this project. One approach was to use the land where this is going to be build, the problem with this is that it's not very evocative. The second alternative was to use images of Squares & Plazas around California, at the end this was the one selected.

The Solution

HERO SECTION

As the website will be the main channel of communication with the community, it is important that visitors provide their contact information. The CTA element must be noticeable.

EVENTS SECTION

This section presents detailed information on upcoming events, as well as materials that have been shared with attendees at previous events.

OPPORTUNITIES SECTION

This section presents alternatives so that users who have not been able to attend the events can get involved with the project.

SUBSCRIPTION FORM

CTA in the hero section leads to this form. Looking for a high conversion rate, it was decided that the subscription form requested the least amount of information possible.
